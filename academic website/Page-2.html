About Me

Hey! It's nice connecting with you here. I am Ruiyao WANG. I am currently a Ph.D. student in the Department of Communication at Cornell University. I acquired my MA in the Chinese University of Hong Kong in Communication, and BA in English Language (Linguistic & New Media and Internet) in Zhejiang University of Finance and Econommics.  

My research focuses on media and its role played in shaping the experience of female and migrants, specifically focusing on the East Asian and Asian female migrants. I am currently working on projects aiming to understand how digital platform and popular media are being used and served as a sphere for female migrants from China and leading a project focusing on the maternal communication of female refugee in Hong Kong.

Ongoing Projects

Open for Collaboration

A-S. Motherhood

This is an on-going project focusing on the experience of asylum seeker mother in Hong Kong and their unique understanding and stories about their past, current and future, and most importantly how they strategically communicate them to their children. This is more than a research, but a story that seeks to link the forced migrants' past, the current Hong Kong, and their unseen future. Contact us to learn more about the project and opportunity for collaborations.

Popular Feminism on Chinese digital platform

Open for Collaboration
Recruitment for informants is ongoing

The rise of digital platforms has significantly influenced the discourse on feminism in China, with platforms like Xiao Hong Shu becoming pivotal spaces for feminist content creators. This project explores the interaction between these creators and the platform, focusing on how popular feminism manifests, evolves, and entangles with the digital ecosystem. We are now welcoming more content creators who are working on feminist topics on the platform to join this conversation.
